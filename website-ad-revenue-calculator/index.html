The Complete Guide to Website Ad Revenue

Everything a content creator needs to understand how display advertising works, what drives your earnings, and how to grow your income over time.

💡

What is RPM? Revenue Per Mille Explained

RPM stands for Revenue Per Mille - "mille" being the Latin word for one thousand. It is the single most useful metric for any ad-supported website owner to understand.

RPM - What You Actually Earn

RPM tells you how much money you earn for every 1,000 visitors or pageviews your site receives. If your RPM is $10 and you have 50,000 monthly visitors, you can expect to earn roughly $500 per month. The formula is:

RPM = (Total Earnings / Total Pageviews) x 1,000

RPM vs CPM - What is the Difference?

CPM (Cost Per Mille) is the price an advertiser pays per 1,000 ad impressions - it is the buying side of the equation. RPM is what a publisher (you) actually receives per 1,000 visitors, after the ad network takes its revenue share. That cut typically ranges from 20% to 45% depending on the platform. Think of CPM as the sticker price and RPM as what lands in your pocket after the middleman takes their fee.

Why Your Niche Has a Massive Impact on Earnings

Not all website traffic is worth the same to advertisers. Ad revenue is determined by what brands are willing to pay to reach your specific audience - and that varies enormously by topic.

The Finance Premium

A personal finance blog can earn 3 to 4 times more per visitor than a general entertainment site. Why? Because a reader browsing articles about mortgages, credit cards, or investment accounts is about to make a high-value financial decision. A bank or insurance company that converts that reader into a customer can generate thousands of dollars in lifetime value, which justifies a very high ad spend.

Tech and Software

Tech content attracts B2B advertisers and SaaS companies with large marketing budgets. A developer who converts into a software subscription could be worth hundreds of dollars in recurring revenue to an advertiser - so they pay a premium to reach that reader.

Entertainment and Gaming

These niches attract enormous audiences, but the advertisers targeting them have smaller budgets and lower per-conversion values. High volume is the lever here. A gaming site at 500,000 monthly visitors can still generate excellent revenue - the math just requires more pageviews to reach the same dollar figure as a smaller finance site.

Why Traffic Location Changes Everything

Where your readers come from is nearly as important as how many you have. The difference between Tier 1 and global traffic can mean a 3x to 5x difference in your monthly earnings at identical traffic levels.

What is Tier 1 Traffic?

Tier 1 refers to visitors from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia. These countries have the most mature digital advertising ecosystems, the highest consumer spending power, and the largest number of advertisers competing for audience attention. More competition means higher bids, higher CPMs, and higher RPMs for publishers.

Why Ad Networks Prioritize US Traffic

Premium ad networks like Raptive and Mediavine are built around relationships with major US advertisers - national brands running programmatic display campaigns. Those advertisers are primarily targeting US consumers, so a site with 80%+ US traffic is significantly more attractive to their advertiser pool than a site with the same visitor count spread across dozens of countries.

What to Do If Your Audience is Global

Do not be discouraged - global traffic can still generate meaningful ad revenue, especially at scale. Focus on growing total traffic volume, and consider whether your content strategy could attract more English-language Tier 1 search traffic. Targeting English keywords naturally tends to skew toward Tier 1 visitors over time.

The Ad Network Upgrade Path: From Zero to Premium

Most successful publishing businesses follow a clear monetization progression. Here is how to think about each stage - and when to make the move.

1

Getting Started - No Traffic Minimum Required

Google AdSense Ezoic

When you are launching a new site, Google AdSense and Ezoic welcome you with no minimum traffic requirement. AdSense is the world's largest ad network, it is straightforward to set up, and it establishes your baseline monetization from day one. Ezoic builds on that by using machine learning to automatically test thousands of ad layout combinations - most publishers see a 20-30% lift over a standard AdSense setup without doing any manual work. Start with AdSense to get familiar with display advertising, and consider Ezoic once you want to optimize your earnings without spending hours on manual testing.

2

Early Growth - 1,000 to 10,000 Monthly Sessions

Journey by Mediavine

Reaching 1,000 monthly sessions is a real milestone - it means your content is finding an audience. At this point, Journey by Mediavine opens its doors. Journey is Mediavine's entry-level program designed specifically for growing publishers who are not yet ready for the full Mediavine platform. It gives you access to Mediavine's ad technology stack at a smaller scale, with better RPMs than basic AdSense. Think of Journey as your on-ramp to the premium tier - a deliberate step up that rewards you for your early growth.

3

Mid-Tier - 10,000 to 25,000 Monthly Pageviews

Monumetric SHE Media

With a solid, established audience, you now qualify for dedicated support and better ad relationships. Monumetric (starting at 10,000 pageviews) specializes in display advertising for mid-size publishers and provides hands-on account support - a noticeable improvement over self-service platforms. SHE Media (starting at 20,000 pageviews) is the standout choice for creators in women's lifestyle, parenting, beauty, or home niches, offering premium brand partnership opportunities alongside standard display advertising. Both networks typically outperform basic Ezoic at this traffic level.

4

Premium Tier - 25,000+ Monthly Pageviews

Raptive

This is where ad monetization becomes genuinely serious for independent publishers. Raptive (formerly AdThrive) is widely considered one of the most trusted and highest-paying premium ad networks for content creators. Acceptance is selective - Raptive evaluates content quality, traffic consistency, and audience composition - but publishers who qualify consistently report RPMs well above what they earned on mid-tier networks. Being accepted into Raptive is a significant validation of your publishing business, and it typically represents a meaningful step up in monthly income for creators who make the switch.

5

Enterprise Scale - 100,000+ Monthly Pageviews

Setupad Full Mediavine

At enterprise scale, you have maximum negotiating leverage and the most to gain from advanced optimization. Setupad specializes in header bidding - a technology that runs multiple simultaneous ad auctions for each available impression, driving up the final sale price. Sites at this level should also explore direct advertising deals, sponsored content partnerships, and programmatic floor price management alongside their primary ad network. When you are generating 100,000+ monthly pageviews, a single percentage-point improvement in RPM can translate to thousands of dollars in additional annual revenue - making sophisticated optimization very much worth the investment.
